International Science and Engineering 
Fair (ISEF) 
10/2019 - 3/2020

The Intel International Science and Engineering Fair, a program of Society for Science & the Public, is the worldâ€™s largest international pre-college science competition. Through a global network of local, regional, and national science fairs, top students are selected from millions, all who have demonstrated their knowledge of science and engineering to further their understanding of the world and improve the way we work and live. Our project aimed for removing micro-plastics from water and reusing them for a greater good for humanity. We did this project because microplastics harm the aquatic life directly and humans indirectly. Also, microplastics might be collected after removal to be recycled, thus, benefit Egypt and other countries. This will be done by taking advantage of the photo-catalysis process that occurs naturally. The process involves the sun degrading micro-plastics naturally into compounds of oxygen and Carbon dioxide. Low density polyethylene type of micro-plastics uses sunlight to degrade into lower forms. The disadvantage in this process is that it takes up to 20 years. So, the main idea of our project is to speed up the previous process by adding a catalyst that will make the process end in hours instead of years. Nano rods from zinc oxide will be used. They will stick to the microplastic and absorb more sunlight and U.V rays which will make the electrons excite and react faster. The chosen process is non pollutant to the actual water, it does not affect the waterâ€™s PH scale and after the effectiveness was measured, its estimated value was about 99%.
We have been qualified from the local and regional stages and become national finalist, but the competition has been canceled due to the  COVID-19 pandemic .

NGN (Next generation Nations) Hackathon. 6/2020 â€“ 7/2020

This hackathon was responsible for finding ways to reduce the effect of COVID-19 or COVID-19 spread by using new technologies. 
  our project was about providing two services, COVID-19 checker, and ordinary symptom checker. If the COVID-19 option is chosen, a questionnaire concerning the symptoms they are experiencing is provided. According to this questionnaire, users are categorized into mild or severe. For mild patients, an online video examination is held between a doctor, and accordingly, the doctor determines necessary procedures and medications after examining the patient's symptoms closely in the video meeting. Otherwise, considered severe, If the patient falls under the severe category, the patient will be provided with the available nearby COVID-19 specialized hospitals to immediately go to and a map API will assist him/her by providing the necessary route to the health care facility. If the user chooses the ordinary symptom checker, the user will be redirected to another page that contains medical categories each presenting a group of body parts/part, such as bone or nose and ears. After choosing the category, a questionnaire is provided to be fill. If the patient is identified as mild, an online video examination is held between the doctor for a precise diagnosis. If identified as severe, he will be provided with the vacant hospitals nearby with its working hours and available specializations and costs. The application has some distinctive features, medications scheduled reminders prescribed by the doctor, a status checker checking the patients' status, and a healing process.

Our prototype was divided into two parts: -
1- A website (web development)
2- An Android app for people who use phones (android and iOS app development).
